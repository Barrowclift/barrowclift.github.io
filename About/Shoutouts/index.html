Code

I am genuinely grateful for all the open source projects and tips I utilized from the Internet community including:

  • BigFoot for their Instapaper-like footer popups use to power my article footnotes.
  • CSSStickyFooter for their wonderful sticky footer solution use for my copyright banner on the bottom of every page.
  • jQueryScript.net for their on-hover unique anchor links solution use for my article section headers.
  • Michael J. Deeb for his help with getting a custom domain set up with Github Pages and Hover.
  • Pygments for providing the syntax highlighting use for my code blocks
  • richleland on Github for his Monokai syntax style for Pygments which I use for my syntax highlighting (with some slight modifications).
  • SimpleForm for providing the contact form backend used on my Contact page.

Design and Writing

I drew inspiration for my design and writing from many professionals including:

  • Matt Gemmell, a man who writes truly captivating and moving pieces on his personal life. Aside from his fantastic personal writings, his remarkable website design served as the core inspiration in my design process. Please support his work.
  • Marco Arment, whose (ahem) salty personality and deep passion for being down-to-earth in his work awoke in me the desire to share my own thoughts and feelings on technology news.
  • John Siracusa, one of the most beloved members of the Mac community. His meticulous attention to detail and hyper-critical nature helped sharpen my own critical eye.
  • Casey Liss, the much-appreciated, grounded voice in the technology writing space whose writings on life as a new father as well as his fun but tasteful website inspired me to keep the human element strong in all I do ("Liss" truly Is More, thanks Casey).
  • James Long, a Firefox developer whose tasteful typography inspired my font choices.

Above all else I thank my Mom and Dad, my older sister Aimee, and my younger brother Michael for their continued support as I pursue my dreams. I love you all, thanks for everything.
